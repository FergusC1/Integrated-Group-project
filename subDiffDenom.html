YEAR 5 FRACTIONS - Adding and Subtracting with Different Denominators

How to add fractions with different Denominators

  1. Check denominators
  2. Find the LCM (Lowest common multiple) for the denominators.

    3. The lowest common multiple is the smallest number two numbers can divide into.

    For this equation we need to find the LCM for 4 and 5.

    Multiples of 5:

    5, 10, 15, 20

    Multiples of 4:

    4, 8, 12, 16, 20

    The LCM for both 4 and 5 is 20

  3. Now we need to make 20 the denominator for both fractions in our example. Lets look at the first fraction.

    4. To get from 4 to 20 we mltiplied 4 by 5.

    As we have multiplied the denominator by 5 we need to do the same for the numerator, so we also multiply 2 by 5.

    Now lets do the same for our second fraction. To get from 5 to 20 we need to multiply 5 by 4 and as we are multiplying our denominator by 4 we also need to multiply our numerator by 4 aswell.

  4. Now we have our common denominator we can add both numerators together to get our result.
  5. Now we have our result we can simplify.

    6. Both numbers can go into 2 so divide both our numerator 18 and our denominator 20 by 2 to get our final answer.
